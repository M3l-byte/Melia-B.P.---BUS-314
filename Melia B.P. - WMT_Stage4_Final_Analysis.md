# **C. Interpretation & Key Findings**

The following five findings represent the most material insights from this analysis and are what a CFO would prioritize in strategic discussions:

**Finding 1 — Walmart Is a Significant Value Creator**

With an EVA of $14.0 billion and MVA of $686.1 billion, Walmart is unambiguously generating returns above its cost of capital. ROC of 20.2% comfortably exceeds the 7.6% WACC, meaning every dollar invested in the business is generating meaningful economic profit. The market-to-book ratio of 9.18x reflects investor conviction in the company's competitive moat and earnings durability.

**Finding 2 — Liquidity Ratios Are Low but Not a Red Flag**

The current ratio of 0.84x and quick ratio of 0.23x appear alarming in isolation but are entirely consistent with Walmart's business model. The company operates with negative net working capital (-$15.0B) because accounts payable ($56.8B) exceed inventories ($56.4B). This means suppliers effectively finance Walmart's inventory — a hallmark of its bargaining power. With $36.4B in operating cash flow and $12.7B in free cash flow, near-term liquidity risk is minimal.

**Finding 3 — Profitability Is Lean but Structurally Sound**

Net profit margin of 2.85% is thin but entirely consistent with Walmart's everyday-low-price (EDLP) strategy. What matters more is that ROC of 20.2% and ROA of 9.25% are strong — Walmart earns superior returns not through wide margins but through extraordinary asset velocity (turnover of 2.80x). This is the correct model for a mass-market retailer.

**Finding 4 — Leverage Is Moderate and Well-Managed**

The debt-to-equity ratio of 0.43x is conservative for a company of Walmart's scale and stability. Times interest earned of 10.76x and cash coverage of 15.52x indicate ample capacity to service existing debt. The total debt ratio of 66.9% is elevated, but a significant portion reflects operating trade payables, not financial debt. The company maintains meaningful capacity to take on additional debt if needed for strategic investment.

**Finding 5 — Efficiency Is Best-in-Class**

An average collection period of just 4.3 days and days in inventory of 40.4 days reflect Walmart's supply chain mastery. Asset turnover of 2.80x is exceptional. These efficiency metrics are foundational to the company's ability to generate strong returns on thin margins — and are difficult for competitors to replicate given the scale required.

# **D. Du Pont Analysis**

The Du Pont decomposition breaks ROE into five multiplicative components, revealing the specific drivers of shareholder returns. For Walmart FY2025:

| Du Pont Component | Value | Interpretation |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Operating Profit Margin | 4.31% | Low margin — reflects EDLP strategy, not inefficiency |
| Asset Turnover | 2.80x | Very high — the engine of Walmart's return model |
| Debt Burden | 90.7% | \~9.3% of EBIT consumed by net interest; modest impact |
| Leverage (Assets/Equity) | 3.02x | Amplifies returns; manageable given stable cash flows |
| Tax Burden | 76.6% | Retains 76.6 cents per dollar of pre-tax income |
| Du Pont ROE | 25.30% | Closely matches directly computed ROE of 25.34% ✓ |

**Key Du Pont Takeaways**

Asset turnover (2.80x) is the dominant driver of ROE. Walmart does not rely on wide margins to generate shareholder returns — it generates them by moving massive volumes through an efficiently managed asset base. This is a structurally sustainable model: it rewards scale, supply chain excellence, and operational discipline rather than pricing power alone.

Leverage (3.02x) provides a secondary amplification effect, contributing meaningfully to the ROE outcome. Importantly, this leverage level is appropriate given Walmart's predictable cash flows, strong interest coverage (TIE of 10.76x), and investment-grade credit profile.

Margin is the component with the most room for improvement. Even a modest improvement in operating margin — from 4.31% to 5.0% through SG\&A rationalization or higher-margin product mix — would produce a meaningful increase in ROE without requiring additional leverage or capital deployment.

# **G. Executive Justification**

**Financial Health Assessment**

Walmart demonstrates robust financial health across all critical dimensions. Net income attributable to Walmart grew 25.3% year-over-year to $19.4B, driven by revenue growth of 5.1% to $685.4B. ROC of 20.2% substantially exceeds the 7.6% WACC, confirming that the business continues to create economic value at scale.

**Operational Efficiency Opportunities**

Asset turnover of 2.80x and inventory days of 40.4 represent best-in-class operational efficiency. While these metrics are already strong, continued investment in supply chain automation and AI-driven demand forecasting could further compress inventory days, freeing billions in working capital. The average collection period of 4.3 days reflects a predominantly cash-and-card sales model that minimizes credit risk.

**Capital Structure Observations**

With a debt-to-equity ratio of 0.43x and TIE of 10.76x, Walmart's balance sheet is conservatively structured relative to its earnings power. The company generated $36.4B in operating cash flow and $12.7B in free cash flow in FY2025, providing ample coverage for both debt service and shareholder returns (dividends of $6.6B plus $4.5B in buybacks). There is meaningful, untapped debt capacity available for strategic deployment.

**Liquidity Position**

While traditional liquidity ratios (current ratio: 0.84x; quick ratio: 0.23x) appear weak by textbook standards, Walmart's negative working capital model is a deliberate strategic advantage. Suppliers extend favorable payment terms because Walmart's purchasing scale is irreplaceable. The risk profile is therefore far lower than the ratios suggest in isolation. Free cash flow of $12.7B and revolving credit facilities provide ample short-term flexibility.

**Value Creation Track Record**

An EVA of $14.0B and MVA of $686.1B confirm that Walmart consistently creates value above and beyond its cost of capital. The market-to-book ratio of 9.18x reflects investor pricing of durable competitive advantages: global scale, supply chain excellence, private label penetration, and growing omnichannel capabilities including Walmart+. These moats support sustained value creation and justify the premium valuation.

**Accounting & Reporting Implications**

The adoption of ASC 842 (lease accounting) has brought significant operating and finance lease obligations onto the balance sheet — $12.9B in long-term operating lease obligations and $4.8B in finance lease obligations as of FY2025. Analysts should account for these in leverage assessments. The effective tax rate of 23.38% is consistent with historical levels and reflects Walmart's global tax optimization structure. No material off-balance-sheet items were identified beyond those already captured under ASC 842\.
